Kampot pepper, steeped in a rich history

stems back to the majestic 12th century Angkorian Empire.


French colonizers played a pivotal role in expanding the trade, 

ensuring its exceptional quality even with production volumes of 8 tonns per year.

        
          The devastating genocidal Khmer Rouge regime in 1975 

          led to its disappearance for nearly three decades.
          Since 2000, with the support of the international community,
          
          this renowned spice has been making a remarkable comeback, 
          
          gracing the plates of the finest global restaurants.


          It now regularly features on menus of top Michelin starred restaurants globally.
